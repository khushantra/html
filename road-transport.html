Road Transportation

Learn about various road-based transportation options and their environmental impact.

Fuel Vehicles

Fuel-powered vehicles emit CO₂, nitrogen oxides, and other pollutants. Their environmental impact varies based on fuel type and efficiency.

  • Fuel Type: Gasoline, Diesel
  • CO₂ Emission: ~170-250 grams per kilometer
  • Fuel Efficiency: ~8-12 L/100 km

Electric Vehicles

Electric vehicles (EVs) produce zero tailpipe emissions, offering a cleaner alternative to gasoline and diesel cars.

  • CO₂ Emission: ~0 grams per kilometer (depending on electricity source)
  • Fuel Efficiency: ~15-20 kWh/100 km

Bicycles

Bicycles are a zero-emission form of transport, with the only environmental cost being the energy used for manufacturing and maintenance.

  • CO₂ Emission: 0 grams per kilometer
  • Fuel Efficiency: N/A (human-powered)

Buses

Buses are a more eco-friendly option than cars, as they can transport many passengers at once, reducing individual emissions.

  • CO₂ Emission: ~60-100 grams per passenger-kilometer
  • Fuel Efficiency: Varies, but generally more efficient than cars per passenger
