Water Transportation

Discover the environmental impact of various water-based transportation methods such as ships, ferries, and boats.

Container Ships

Container ships are vital for global trade, but they also contribute significantly to CO₂ emissions.

  • CO₂ Emission: ~15-25 grams per ton-mile
  • Fuel Type: Heavy fuel oil (HFO)
  • Impact: Major contributor to marine pollution and global warming

Cruise Ships

Cruise ships carry passengers for long-distance travel. Their environmental impact is high due to their large size and luxury amenities.

  • CO₂ Emission: ~250-300 grams per passenger-mile
  • Fuel Type: Diesel, Heavy fuel oils
  • Impact: High emissions, ocean pollution

Ferries

Ferries offer short-distance travel across bodies of water, but their environmental impact depends on fuel used.

  • CO₂ Emission: ~50-150 grams per passenger-mile
  • Fuel Type: Diesel, Natural Gas
  • Impact: Moderate emissions, but electric ferries are a cleaner option

Small Boats

Small boats, used for recreational purposes or fishing, can have varying impacts depending on fuel type and engine size.

  • CO₂ Emission: Varies depending on the boat's engine size
  • Fuel Type: Gasoline, Diesel, Electric
  • Impact: Smaller boats have localized pollution, but electric boats are emerging as cleaner alternatives
